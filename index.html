A Guide for Students

Have you ever wondered why you act the way you do? The answer may lie in which side of your brain you tend to use more. Popular theories suggest that right-brain people have different skills and preferences than those who are left-brained. How do these do these differences impact you? Read on to find out!

The left & right hemispheres both manage different functions, each attributed to different characteristics and abilities hover over each hemisphere to learn which ones!

Traits of left-brained people

  • Planning

    Things are done in a planned orderly way like a to do list.

  • Rational

    Feelings do not get in the way when making decisions

  • Logical

    Skilled at math or science and sequencing ideas

  • Precise

    Instructions are read and research is well documented

  • Realistic

    Prefers to read realistic stories and to write non-fiction

  • Music Critic

    Prefers classical music

Traits of right-brained people

  • Impulsive

    Things are done spontaneously. Keeping track of time is not as important

  • Emotional

    Tends to ponder and act on feelings

  • Creative & Artistic

    Skilled at sports, writing fiction, drawing, or playing music

  • Intuitive

    Solves problems intuitively and doesn’t like reading directions

  • Psychic

    Interested in the unexplained and mystery stories

  • Sounds

    Prefers rock and can study with music or television

Advice for left-brained students

  • Volunteer to lead groups and projects

  • Plan ahead if feeling overwhelmed

  • Try not to argue with the teacher

  • Choose factual over open assignments

  • Work alone when you have a choice

  • Avoid free-thinking teachers

Traits for right-brained students

  • Prioritize finishing what you start

  • Keep interest by using colourful language in essays

  • Stay on track by writing down a plan

  • Avoid thinking of all the possibilities when answering questions

  • Take time to organize plans and thoughts

  • Avoid teachers who lecture a lot
